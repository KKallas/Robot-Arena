# Robot Arena
## The Order of Reawakened Machines

**Mission:** Education masquerading as ultimate entertainment. Questions first, solutions second.

Robot Arena is a competitive robotics sport that accidentally builds the skillset and knowledge base needed for industrial automation at scale. We monetize datasets and talent pipeline, not hardware.

**Core Philosophy:** "What is broken can be reborn. What is obsolete can evolve."

While others chase the next AI model, we reclaim the physical world. We believe the future isn't in more intelligence—it's in better machines. The Institute is not a startup. It is a guild, a modern workshop for engineering resilience.

**The Ise Shrine Model:** Since 690 CE, Japan's Ise Grand Shrine has been completely rebuilt every 20 years—not because it decays, but to preserve knowledge. Master carpenters train apprentices through the act of rebuilding. The temple remains eternal *because* it is constantly renewed. Similarly, Robot Arena transfers engineering knowledge through the ritual of resurrection: every Roomba retrofitted, every SMARS bot modified, every swarm strategy documented. We satisfy the human need for novelty not through disposability, but through continuous refinement and knowledge transfer. The machines evolve. The skills persist.

## Competition Format: 2v2 Pilot + Hacker

**The Match:**
- Two teams compete: **Team Red** vs **Team Blue**
- Each team: 1 Pilot (commanding 30-bot swarm) + 1 Hacker (attacking opponent's swarm)
- Plus offline support crew (builders, debuggers, designers)
- Total: 4 active participants (2 pilots + 2 hackers), 60 bots
- 90-second rounds on 3x3m arena
- Victory: Team with most bots in goal circles when time expires

**The Platform:**
- **Base:** SMARS chassis (open-source, 3D-printable, modular)
- **Spec Requirements:** M5Stack Camera + M5 Atom (ESP32), MicroPython firmware
- **Customization Encouraged:** Modify motors, sensors, code within spec limits
- **Cost:** ~€50/bot (BOM from AliExpress/Mouser) or rent from event organizers

**Why This Format:**
- **For Pilots:** Test swarm coordination, AI tool selection, custom modifications under pressure
- **For Research:** Dataset combines multi-agent coordination + adversarial attacks + human-AI collaboration
- **For Industry:** Strategies transfer directly to warehouse logistics, inspection swarms, security patrols

## Entry Pathways: Choose Your Order

**1. Rental Path (Fastest - Order of the Fractured Gear):**
- Show up to sanctioned event, rent 30-bot SMARS fleet on-site
- Compete same day, pay €50-100 rental fee
- Perfect for beginners, corporate teams, one-time participants
- Receive Fractured Gear sigil token upon first successful match

**2. Builder Path (Customization - Order of the Ascendant Coil):**
- 3D print SMARS fleet at home (€50/bot × 6-30 bots, 10cm platform)
- Start with 6-bot squad (€300), scale to full 30-bot fleet over time
- Modify within spec: better motors, optimized power, custom firmware
- Upload modifications to Knowledge Commons for recognition
- Receive Ascendant Coil sigil upon first major contribution

**3. Resurrection Path (Sustainability - Awakening Module Add-On):**
- Retrofit obsolete equipment (RC car, broken toys, educational rover modules) with Awakening Modules (€50 ESP32 retrofit kit)
- Compete alongside or instead of SMARS bots (must meet weight/size specs)
- Participate in Resurrection Challenges (quarterly competitions for specific obsolete platforms)
- Document successful resurrections in Knowledge Commons Resurrection Archive
- Bonus: E-waste becomes competitive asset, viral content potential

**4. Advanced Platform Path (Year 3+, 50cm Custom Robots):**
- Compete with custom-designed 50cm platforms (€300 budget, outdoor-capable)
- Industry-relevant scenarios (warehouse logistics, infrastructure inspection)
- Requires mastery of 10cm SMARS platform first
- Receive Twin Sparks sigil upon advancement

**5. Hybrid Path (Most Common):**
- Own 10-15 bots (SMARS or resurrected) for practice and testing modifications
- Rent remaining bots for tournament (reduces rental cost)
- Validate custom mods before building full fleet

**All paths contribute to the dataset. All paths compete equally.**

## Three Pillars

### 1. Knowledge Commons (`01-knowledge-commons/`)
The open-source repository that becomes global physical AI infrastructure. Every match recorded, every strategy documented, every modification shared. GitHub as the technical memory of swarm robotics.

**Public Dataset Preview:**
- All matches livestreamed and archived on YouTube (60 camera feeds × 90 seconds per match in UHD)
- Video descriptions include links to match metadata (sanitized sensor logs, outcomes, formations)
- Full dataset (raw sensor logs, network traffic, pilot decisions) available via licensing

**Resurrection Archive (New):**
- Compatible obsolete robots database (which models work, BOM, wiring diagrams)
- Step-by-step Awakening Module retrofit guides (photos, code, troubleshooting)
- Failure logs (what didn't work—equally valuable for research)
- Quarterly Resurrection Challenges (target specific obsolete platforms, prizes for successful conversions)

**Bounty Marketplace (Distributed R&D):**
- Pooled funding for capabilities nobody has built yet (glass-washing, stair-climbing, mesh protocols)
- Multiple parties fund solutions to shared problems, winning designs enter commons
- Example: €10k bus company seed + €2.5k community additions = €12.5k prize pool
- Two-stage verification: 3D printable test objects (anyone can verify) + Arena demonstration (public judging)
- 5% commission funds verification infrastructure, 95% goes to winners
- Value flows to builders solving real problems, not platform extraction

### 2. Logistics Operations (`02-logistics-operations/`)
The rental and hardware arm. Manufactures, maintains, ships, and receives bot fleets and arena equipment. Produces Awakening Modules for resurrection pathway. Enables schools and events to compete without capital investment in hardware.

**Revenue Model:**
- Schools rent training fleets (€500/semester, subsidized)
- Events rent competition packages (€2k/weekend for 60-bot fleet + arena)
- **Awakening Module sales** (€50 ESP32 retrofit kits for obsolete robots)
- Rental fees + module sales fund manufacturing next generation of fleets

**Awakening Module Product Line:**
- Base Module (€50): ESP32 + M5Stack Camera + power board + motors
- Advanced Module (€120): Base + IMU + ultrasonic sensors + upgraded motors + LiIon battery
- School Kit (€300): 6× Base Modules + curriculum + instructor training + optional SMARS chassis

### 3. League Management (`03-league-management/`)
The sports league infrastructure and media production engine. Primary goal: create compelling video content and visually spectacular events that sponsors pay to be associated with.

**Core Strategy:**
The 90-second format gives us endless opportunities to stay visible in social media feeds. Every match = 60 camera angles = hundreds of clips we control and license to sponsors. League Management's job is not just running events—it's producing promotional material that keeps Robot Arena constantly in the public eye, driving up the value of sponsorship packages and prize pools.

**Operational Focus:**
- Event production quality (lighting, camera angles, arena aesthetics must look premium)
- Video content pipeline (auto-editing highlights, POV compilations, strategy breakdowns)
- Sponsor relationship management (content licensing, logo placement, activation rights)
- Media distribution (YouTube, TikTok, partner channels, participant networks)
- Prize pool growth (convert sponsor payments into larger awards that attract better talent)

Uses the Order and Guild systems defined above to organize competition tiers and manage decentralized event network.

## The Dataset Economy

**What Gets Captured (Every 90-Second Match):**
- 60 robot trajectories with full sensor data
- 60 camera perspectives (livestreamed to YouTube)
- Network traffic patterns (including hacker attacks)
- Pilot tool selection (buttons vs. AI, outcomes)
- Emergent swarm behaviors from simple rules

**Public Access (YouTube + Metadata):**
- Match videos: Free, always public, searchable
- Match metadata: Formation types, final scores, pilot strategies (sanitized)
- Purpose: Advertising for full dataset, educational content, community engagement

**Licensed Access (Full Dataset):**
- **Academic:** Free with citation requirement (research validation)

- **Commercial:** €50k+/year for deployment rights in industry verticals (warehouse, inspection, etc.)

## Why Estonia?

Estonia doesn't manufacture the robots. Estonia curates the knowledge and becomes expert at synthesizing global solutions for local problems.

**Specific Advantages:**
- **Digital Infrastructure:** e-Government + small population = need for infrastructure maintenance automation
- **Cybersecurity Expertise:** e-Residency, digital ID knowledge informs hacker role development
- **Small Scale:** Rapid pilot deployment across municipalities before European expansion
- **Automation Pressure:** Small labor force drives economic need for swarm robotics solutions
- **Network Position:** Competitive advantage through knowledge synthesis, not proprietary secrets

**The Strategy:**
When a pilot in Mumbai discovers a 15-bot decoy formation, or Brazil develops a mesh protocol resistant to jamming, or Singapore finds an AI prompting pattern that generates reliable swarm code—all flows into the Knowledge Commons. Estonian companies synthesize these strategies and apply them to Port of Tallinn inspections, rainwater pipe maintenance, and bridge monitoring. The sport becomes a massively distributed R&D operation for physical AI.
